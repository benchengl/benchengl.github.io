Homotopy

A homotopy is any continuous family of maps \(F_t:X\rightarrow Y, t\in [0,1]\). Call two functions \(f,g:X\rightarrow X\) homotopic, denoted as \(f\simeq g\) if there is a homotopy \(F_t\) that \(F_0=f,F_1=g\). If \(f\) is homotopic to a constant map, then we say \(f\) is nullhomotopic.


A special case of homotopy is deformation retraction which is a continuous family \(F_t:X\rightarrow X\) that for any \(t\), \[F_0=\textrm{Id}_X, F_1(X)=A\subseteq X, F_t|_{A}=\textrm{Id}_A\] Define a retract from \(X\) to \(A\) as a map \[f:X\rightarrow A, f|_A=\textrm{Id}_A\] so a deformation retraction is a homotopy from the identity map to a retraction.
