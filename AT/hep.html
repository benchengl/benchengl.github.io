Homotopy Extension Property

We say a \(X,A\) satisfies homotopy extension property if \((X\times \{0\})\cup (A\times I)\) is a retract of \(X\times I\). Note that it's equivalent to saying that the mapping cylinder \(M_i\) is a retract of \(X\times I\) where \(i:A\rightarrow X\) is the inclusion.

Example: Let \(X\) be a line segement and \(A\) be a point on the line, then \((X,A)\) satisfies the HEP.

Remark: If \((X,A)\) is a CW pair, then \(M_i\) is a deformation retract of \(X\times I\), which implies that \((X,A)\) satisfies HEP. Here is the proof.
