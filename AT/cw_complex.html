CW Complex

Briefly speaking, a CW complex is just a space constructed by intuitively gluing \(n\)-cells or disks \(e^n\) to a \(n-1\) dimensional space. The procedures are as follows:

  • Start with a set of points \(X^0\)
  • Inductively form \(X^n\) from \(X^{n-1}\) by attaching \(e_{\alpha}^n\) via maps \(\psi_{\alpha}:S^{n-1}\rightarrow X^{n-1}\). The map explains where the boundary of cells goes in \(X^{n-1}\).
  • The process stops at some \(n\). \(A\) is said to be open in \(X\) iff \(A\cap X^n\) is open for each \(n\)

Example: A torus has the cell structure \(e^0\cup e_1^1\cup e^1_2\cup e^2\).


A subcomplex \(A\subset X\) is a closed subspace that's a union of cells. They together form a CW pair \((X,A)\).
