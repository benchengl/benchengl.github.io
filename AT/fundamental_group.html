Fundamental Group

A path in \(X\) is a continuous map \(f:I\rightarrow X\) and a homotopy of paths is a continuous family \(F_t:I\times X\) that \(F_0,F_1\) are independent of \(t\). We will exlpain in this proof why this homotopy of paths is an equivalence relation. Denote \([f]\) as the homotopy class of \(f\). The fundamental group with basepoint \(x\) denoted as \(\pi_1(X,x_0)\) is defined as the set of all homotopy classes \([f]\) where \(f\) is a loop or a path with same endpoints at \(x\).

Example: \(\pi_1(S^1)=\mathbb{Z}\). See the proof here.

A space is simply connected iff there is a unique homotopy class of paths between any two points.

Remark: For two path-connected spaces, the fundamental group of their cartesian product is just the cartesian product of their fundamental group. See proof here.
