Covering Space

A covering space of \(X\) denoted as \(\tilde{X}\) is a space with a covering map \(p:\tilde{X}\rightarrow X\) such that for each \(x\in X\), there is an open neighborhood \(U\) of \(x\) in \(X\) that \(p^{-1}(U)\) is a union of disjoint open sets and each of them is mapped homeomorphically to \(U\) via \(p\). Such \(U\) is called evenly covered. Disjoint open sets homeomorphic to \(U\) is sheets of \(\tilde{X}\) over \(U\).

Example: \(p:\mathbb{R}\rightarrow S^1, p(x)=(\cos 2\pi x, \sin 2\pi x)\) is a covering map and any open arc on \(S^1\) is evenly covered.

Remark: Number of sheets of a covering map \(p:\tilde{X}\rightarrow X\) with \(X,\tilde{X}\) path-connected equals the index of \(p_{\ast}(\pi_1(\tilde{X}))\) in \(\pi_1(X)\).

Let \(p:\tilde{X}\rightarrow X\) be a covering map and \(f:Y\rightarrow X\) a continuous map, then a lift of \(f\) is \(\tilde{f}:Y\rightarrow \tilde{X}\) that \(f=p\circ \tilde{f}\).

Remark: See path lifting property and homotopy lifting property.
